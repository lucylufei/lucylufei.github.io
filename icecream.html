I think I've reached a point where I'm incapable of living without icecream in my life. I admit it's a problem, but not one that's on my priority to deal with. Especially not in Vancouver with all these unique icecream shops that seem to be popping up every week.

Icecream is a great way to bond with people! Grab some friends and head to some of my favourite icecream places.



  Off the Grid  

Off the Grid Waffles is a cute dessert restaurant in East Vancouver featuring overloaded milkshakes in mason jars. It's a little oversweet, but why go for desserts if you're not looking for sweet. I also really like that they have little board games at each table, like Jenga or Connect 4 to keep you occupied.


  Soft Peaks  


  Mister  

Definitely my favourite nitrogen icecream shop by far. It's in a perfect location for desserts after dinner in one of those super nice Yaletown restaurants. The only downside is the lack of seating inside the shop. If there were more seats, I could probably spend hours just watching the nitrogen evaporate off the mixing machines and each creme brulee order being torched to golder perfection.


  Bonne Crepe  

I can't even count the amount of times I've been to this matcha place. Literally every time I've ever walked past it, I cannot resist ordering the icecream. My favourite is the matcha icecream crepe which is definitely hard to find elsewhere. They also have some super cute tables inside, which was unexpected for a grocery store.


  17 Degrees  

I was drawn towards 17 Degrees for their watermelon shaved ice and the uniqueness of light bulb drinks. Both of those things are a little more common now so the allure is less strong. It's definitely a cool place to check out, and the watermelon tastes surprisingly well with the milky shaved ice.


  Chicco  

Not very recommended in my opinion for their parfaits. Then again, my standards for food usually rely solely on the looks. The taste wasn't bad or anything, but not worth the price. Although, I probably just didn't order the right thing.


  Earnest  

Earnest is a good icecream place and everything was absolutely delicious. I think it's a little overhyped for their lack of flavours and options outside of icecream. It's a nice place to stop, but maybe not travelling intentionally just for the icecream.


  On Yogurt  

This was the first rolled icecream place I've ever tried, so mostly I was fascinated with watching the icecream be made. It's a great place to go to get your icecream customized for exactly what you are looking for, but sometimes having that much freedom won't necessarily lead to good choices.


  UYU  

One of the best frozen yogurt places ever. I can't imagine going there when they're busy, but every order is prepared with so much care. Every piece of topping is placed individually! I guess it's a little unnecessary since it all gets eaten anyways, but it sure makes people feel important and fancy.


  Styo  

Styo is the only place where I've had matcha frozen yogurt that was actually frozen yogurt and tasted like yogurt. All their soft serves are super pretty and tasty, but the best things are their popsicles.

Welcome to Hood Hunter


Hood Hunter is an explorer visualization tool to support prospective home buyers in narrowing neighbourhoods for a potential home purchase developed to fulfill the requirements of the CPSC 547 course on data visualization. This project was created by Abiramy Kuganesan, Ivan Song, and Lufei Liu with equal contributions.


Our tool is built within an Observable Notebook and adapted into an HTML/CSS dashboard. The presentation and demo of our tool can also be found here.


The main tasks of Hood Hunter are to filter, compare, and view details. We use crossfilter to filter by home prices, which gives a better overview of the available options. We adapt lineup for comparison to communicate the factors behind the ranking system. This feature also allows sorting by attributes or adjusting weights to better suit personal needs. Lastly, we have a detail pane for the selected location which shows a history of past pricing, helping understand the pricing trend, as well as more information about individual factors.


The data that we are visualizing originates from multiple sources. We use the Canadian census data for criminal code violations as our safety metric; the foundational skills assessment by the province of BC for our education metric; and the proximity database from Statistics canada for all our proximity metrics. Lastly, Housing prices are available from the canadian mortgage and housing corporation for each type of housing in all neighbourhoods with more than 50,000 population.


Hope you enjoy!

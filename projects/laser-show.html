Laser Light Show

ELEC 391 Project


Project Summary

Our goal for this project was to design and build a spherical wrist with two degrees of freedom entirely from scratch. This includes two mechanically commutated, permanent magnet DC motors that are hand-wired with custom parts. The final result can draw a shape on a flat surface using a laser light.

Motor Design

We choose carbon brushes over stranded wire for our motors for durability and a larger surface area to conduct current. We also use rectangular magnets over circular ones for better fit inside our motor chasis and orient them for the largest magnetic flux through the motor.




Design Process

From our initial skectches, we started with a Lego prototype to test out the structure and feasibility. From these, we started building the motors, and testing to ensure they were functional. With working motors, we could then build our circuit and start the PID controller tuning process, which was made more difficult with the imperfections of our handmade motors.
